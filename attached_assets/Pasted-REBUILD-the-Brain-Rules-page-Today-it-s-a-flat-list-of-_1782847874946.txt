THE CORE CHANGE — three sections, because today's toggles are actually different object
types with different risk:

1) AUTOMATIONS · act for you (delegations that remove you from the loop)
   - Rules that auto-approve a narrow class of proposals: pay recurring bills, move extra
     cash to savings, chase overdue invoices, run payroll.
   - Each row shows its SCOPE INLINE (not just a toggle) so a rule is understandable without
     opening it: e.g. "6 trusted vendors · under $1,000 · cleared 14 this month" in mono.
   - A chevron + tap opens the existing RuleDetail. A per-row Active/Paused toggle pauses
     without opening.
   - PAUSED-FROM-REPORT STATE: a rule paused via the report-a-problem flow renders here with
     a #D20344 left-accent and "Paused after you reported a problem · tap to review", toggle
     off. This makes the rules page the single control tower where every automation — including
     stopped ones — is visible.
   - Inline editable amounts (e.g. the $25,000 sweep threshold) use the dashed-underline
     inline-editable style.

2) GUARDRAILS · pull you back in (thresholds, not delegations)
   - Rules like "Ask me before paying over $500". The AMOUNT is the primary control: render
     it as an inline-editable bordered pill (mono) with a small edit affordance, not buried
     in a toggle.
   - Subtext frames it as a protection: "No automation clears anything above this without you."

3) ALWAYS ON · can't be turned off (the non-negotiable guards)
   - "Flag unusual activity", "Stop on changed bank details", "Catch possible duplicates".
   - Render as LOCKED rows: lock icon, an "Always on" label, NO toggle. These are read-only.
   - Caption beneath: "Brain always does these — they're your safety floor and can't be
     disabled, even by a rule."
   - IMPORTANT: this fixes a real problem — "flag strange behavior" must NOT be a toggle a
     user can switch off. Move it out of the toggleable list into here.

- Section "Suggested for you · learned from your history".
- Each suggestion card shows the EVIDENCE that triggered it, in a mono fact block, e.g.:
    you approved  Con Edison, Comcast, Verizon
    11 times      in 3 months
    always        under $600 · never flagged
  plus a confidence label ("Confidence · high"). The evidence is what makes the ML story
