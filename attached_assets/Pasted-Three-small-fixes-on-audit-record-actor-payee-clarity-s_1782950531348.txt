Three small fixes on audit-record actor/payee clarity, surfaced by the payroll record
(AUD-5J7Y) review. git fetch/pull first; work on a new feature branch (e.g.
fix/actor-payee-clarity); track done/pending against this plan; update CLAUDE.md at the end.

CONTEXT: on AUD-5J7Y, "sarah@meridian approved" (the ACTOR — who decided) and "J. Smith
(Engineering)" in linked evidence (the PAYEE — who was paid) are different people, which is
CORRECT (approver ≠ recipient). But the UI doesn't distinguish the two roles, so it reads
ambiguously. Make the roles explicit and encode the segregation-of-duties principle in the
guard.

FIX 1 — ACTOR ROLE LABEL on lifecycle approval steps. Where a lifecycle step shows a human
approval ("sarah@meridian approved"), append the actor's role in muted text:
"sarah@meridian approved · finance admin". Drive this from data (add an actorRole field to the
lifecycle step or resolve it from the member/actor record — whichever fits the current types);
do not hardcode strings per record. Apply across all audit records with human-approval steps.
(Note: this label is also the future home of the authority line from the upcoming members/
limits spec — e.g. "within her $10K payroll limit" — so structure the field to allow an
optional authority suffix, but do NOT build the members model in this pass.)

FIX 2 — RELATIONSHIP LABEL on linked-evidence counterparty rows. The linked-evidence label
should carry the RELATIONSHIP, not just the entity type. On payment records, the receiving
party's row reads "PAYEE" (or "EMPLOYEE · PAID TO" for employee rows) instead of bare
"EMPLOYEE"/"VENDOR". Concretely: AUD-5J7Y's row becomes "PAYEE · J. Smith (Engineering)";
vendor rows on payment records become "PAYEE · {vendor}" or "VENDOR · PAID TO" per the
existing label style — pick one convention and apply it consistently. Non-payment linked rows
(rule, invoice, proposal) are unchanged. Drive from data (a relationship field on the linked[]
entry or derived from record type), not per-surface hardcoding.

FIX 3 — ACTOR ≠ PAYEE ASSERT in the unified dev guard (extend, don't fork). Add a
segregation-of-duties coherence check: flag any audit record where the approving actor and the
receiving counterparty resolve to the same identity (same member/email/entity). console.error
"record → actor==payee (segregation-of-duties)"; never throw; runs at boot with the existing
guard. It should pass clean on current mock data — its job is to catch the future case.

VERIFY: AUD-5J7Y shows "sarah@meridian approved · finance admin" and "PAYEE · J. Smith
(Engineering)"; other payment records show the same convention; the guard passes clean and the
new assert is present.
