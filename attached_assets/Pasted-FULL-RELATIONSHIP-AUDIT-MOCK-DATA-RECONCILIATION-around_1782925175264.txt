STEP 2 — COHERENCE CHECKS (the "resolves-but-lies" class)
=========================================================================
Resolution is not enough. Assert each of these across the graph and list every violation:

AMOUNTS
- invoice.amount == the proposal amount == the audit record amount == the settled card amount,
  for the same lifecycle. No silent drift ($4,150 vs $4,090).
- A sweep/treasury proposal's math reconciles (buffer + pending AP covered; operatingAfter > 0).

VENDOR ↔ TRUST ↔ BEHAVIOR
- The vendor of a proposal exists and its trustStatus is coherent with how the proposal behaved:
  * auto_approved/auto-cleared → vendor is "trusted" AND the clearing rule's allowlist includes
    that vendor AND amount ≤ the rule cap.
  * human-approved-above-limit (e.g. AWS) → vendor is NOT auto-clearing it; do NOT mark it
    "trusted-as-if-auto" just to look tidy. Status matches evidence.
  * flagged/held (e.g. Bright Futures bank-detail change) → vendor is "under_review" EVERYWHERE;
    it must NOT appear as auto_approved in any audit record.
- A kind:"vendor" link must point at an actual vendor. Non-vendor counterparties (payroll
  employees, protocols, ledger) must use an accurate non-vendor kind, not "vendor".
- vendor.ruleIds and rule.allowlist are reciprocal — if a rule lists a vendor, that vendor lists
  the rule, and vice versa.

VENDOR HISTORY ↔ PAYMENTS
- Every vendor's history (paymentCount, totalPaid, avgAmount, first/lastPaidLabel,
  payeeAccountLast4) reconciles with the invoices/payments/audit records that reference it. A
  vendor with a linked paid invoice must have paymentCount ≥ 1 and totals/dates/account that
  reflect it. No "trusted since January, 11 payments" for a vendor whose only referenced record
  is one July invoice; no zero-history vendor sitting behind a paid invoice.

RULES
- Every ruleId referenced resolves to a canonical rule; auto_approved records link a rule whose
  category matches the vendor/invoice category (don't clear a contractor invoice with a lease
  rule); a flagged/held payment carries NO rule link.

LIFECYCLE / STATUS
- A proposal's status and its audit lifecycle agree (pending has no executed/anchor; executed
  has the full proposed→approved→executed timeline; auto_approved shows "approved by rule, no
  human step").
- ANCHOR honesty: pending/executing → "pending_next_batch" (Verify disabled, no hashes);
  executed+anchored → real (truncated) hashes + Verify enabled. Never "anchored" for something
  unsettled.

LINKED-ROW SPLIT
- The old conflated "Proposal · Invoice #…" row is fully gone; audit-record popup, ProposalDetail,
  and SettledRecordCard each render separate Proposal / Invoice / Vendor rows, each calling its
  own resolver (openProposalDetail / openInvoiceDetail / openVendorDetail / openRuleDetail), with
  resolve-or-plain-text fallback, no dead taps.
